Assessment & Activation

90-day deployment to convert policies, training practices, and supervision workflows into working systems that reduce administrative effort and strengthen staff readiness

Opportunities

Three ways to strengthen operations without disrupting care

Reduce time spent on "busy work"

The challenge: Teams lose time to repetitive tasks—documentation review, manual QA checks, and repeated clarification requests on policies and procedures.

What we build: A cited Org Copilot and supervisor visibility views.

What it enables: Faster answers, fewer interruptions, and less manual review burden for routine questions.

Strengthen training consistency—at scale

The challenge: Strong teams benefit from standardized practice for high-frequency and high-risk situations (de-escalation, safety pathways, documentation-critical moments).

What we build: Zia-specific roleplay scenarios with scoring and feedback aligned to your expectations.

What it enables: Consistent practice, clearer evaluation signals, and a repeatable training loop embedded into onboarding.

Improve supervisory visibility

The challenge: Supervision works best when signals arrive early—seeing where support is needed before incidents or disengagement occur.

What we build: Lightweight supervisor visibility views plus behavioral analytics using available signals.

What it enables: Targeted coaching, trend summaries (incident themes, engagement drift), and more proactive supervision.

The Plan

A phased approach that delivers usable tools immediately while building toward full integration

Phase A: Assessment & Activation

Weeks 1–12 | 90 Days Fixed Scope

Discovery (Weeks 1–3): Document collection and policy ingestion kickoff, leadership + frontline interviews, scenario mapping and evaluation alignment, confirm what data/artifacts exist and in what format

Build & Prototype (Weeks 4–10): Org Copilot live (MVDS), roleplay scenarios live (priority set), prototype supervisor visibility views and governance guardrails drafted, weekly feedback cycles with Zia stakeholders

Validation & Handoff (Weeks 11–12): Supervisor and leadership validation, adjustments and refinements, delivery of Phase B annual scope + integration plan + fixed pricing proposal

Phase B: Ongoing Activation & Integration

Annual Program | Pricing Delivered at Day 90

What unlocks: Ongoing policy updates, scenario expansion and continuous improvement, EMR/workflow integration so intelligence appears in the flow of work, automated pipelines that prevent "frozen" analytics

Why it matters: Without Phase B, the system becomes static—policy alignment won't stay current as procedures evolve, scenarios won't refresh based on operational reality, and the system won't reach full impact without workflow integration

What You Get

Tangible deliverables by Day 90

Workstream A: Training & Roleplay Activation

MVDS: Scenario-mapping workshop with leadership + frontline representation, initial set of Zia-specific roleplay scenarios (priority set), scoring rubric + feedback framework aligned to Zia expectations, plan for embedding roleplay into onboarding and annual evaluations

Annual expansion: Continuous scenario expansion by role/location, ongoing rubric calibration, continuous improvement loop driven by training performance and supervisor feedback

Workstream B: Internal AI Copilot (Org Copilot)

MVDS: Ingestion of policies/SOPs/training resources, a private Zia-only Copilot that answers staff questions with exact source citations and links to relevant training, query logging to surface recurring confusion and training needs, lightweight update workflow for policy changes

Annual expansion: Continuous ingestion/versioning of updated policies, steady update cadence so Copilot stays aligned with "how things are done now," trend insights from recurring questions, deeper policy-to-training linkage

Workstream C: Supervision & QA Intelligence

MVDS: Assessment of current supervision and QA workflows, prototype supervisor visibility views highlighting support-needed signals and recurring knowledge gaps, definition of governance guardrails supporting transparency, auditability, clinician trust, and practical privacy expectations

Annual expansion: Move from prototype views to operational dashboards with stable definitions, trend views over time (skills, training coverage, recurring friction points), alert thresholds tuned to avoid fatigue and maintain trust

Workstream D: Integration & Deployment Planning

MVDS: Phased rollout plan by role (clinicians, techs, supervisors) and across locations, workflow recommendations for where Copilot and roleplay live day-to-day, integration roadmap to validate feasibility and sequencing, readiness checklist for broader deployment

Annual expansion: EMR/workflow integration so Copilot, training prompts, and supervisory views appear in the flow of work, automated pipelines that reduce manual exports, permissions/role-based access alignment, ongoing rollout support across locations
